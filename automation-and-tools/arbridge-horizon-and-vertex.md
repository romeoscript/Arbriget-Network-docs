# Arbridge Horizon and Arbridge Vertex

Arbridge offers two operational tiers built on the Pulse engine. They share the same execution intelligence but are tuned for different risk postures, capital profiles, and participation horizons.

## Arbridge Horizon

Horizon is the steady-state tier. It is engineered for members who prioritize consistency and accessibility over aggressive frequency.

- **Target profile:** steady, diversified arbitrage capture across liquid networks
- **Cadence:** continuous, seven days a week
- **Entry:** low-threshold, designed for onboarding members into the Arbridge operational model
- **Strategy focus:** on-chain spread capture and stable cross-chain routes with high probability of settlement

Horizon favors breadth over intensity. It runs the Pulse engine with a conservative risk threshold so that only opportunities with high-probability settlement ever reach execution.

## Arbridge Vertex

Vertex is the advanced tier. It is engineered for members who can commit capital with a defined maturation horizon in exchange for more aggressive opportunity capture.

- **Target profile:** higher-frequency, higher-yield arbitrage and cross-chain execution
- **Minimum participation:** $2,500 USD-equivalent
- **Maturation period:** 75 days before capital becomes eligible for standard withdrawal terms
- **Early-exit fee:** 15% applied to capital withdrawn before maturation
- **Strategy focus:** broader venue coverage, deeper spread capture, active cross-chain route optimization

Vertex unlocks execution surfaces that require sustained capital presence — particularly multi-hop cross-chain flows where route preparation and position pre-staging materially improve outcomes.

## Live transparency

Both tiers surface executions through a Live Execution API. Members can observe trades as they settle on decentralized venues, follow the route graph end-to-end, and reconcile outcomes against the on-chain record — the same record the Arbridge operations desk reconciles against internally.
